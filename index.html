An internal combustion engine is a heat engine in which a fuel burns with an oxidizer (usually air) in the combustion chamber, which is part of the fuel flow circuit. The expansion of high-pressure and high-temperature gases produced by combustion in an internal combustion engine exerts a direct force on some engine components. This force is applied to the pistons, turbine blades, and impulse nozzle. This force moves the part it affects a certain distance as a result of the conversion of chemical energy into mechanical energy. Turbo

by: saif kh
1885

A turbocharger is a gas compressor used to compress air for an internal combustion engine to produce a higher horsepower given the size of the engine. The difference between the turbocharger and the supercharger is that the supercharger rotates mechanically, usually by means of a belt or gear from the crankshaft (crankshaft), while the turbocharger rotates with exhaust gases. The turbocharger increases the entry f air and fuel into the combustion chamber, and turbocharged engines are usually more efficient than supercharged engines, but they are less responsive. Turbochargers are commonly found in truck, car, train and construction equipment engines.

The electric motor

by: saif kh
1821

The electric motor was invented by the English scientist Michael Faraday in 1821. It is one of the devices that are widely used in our modern era, as it drives machines in factories and drives electric trains, as well as operates sewing machines, runs electric washing machines, runs pumps in refrigerators, and others. There are many types of it, large and small, to suit every use.
